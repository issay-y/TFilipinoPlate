Filipino Pork Adobo

A richer, more indulgent version of the classic. Pork belly simmered until the fat renders and the sauce becomes thick and savory.

Prep: 15m
Cook: 1h
Servings: 4-5
Other Adobo styles:
Chicken Adobo Adobong Puti Adobong Sitaw

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Pork Belly (Liempo), cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup Soy Sauce
  • 1/3 cup Cane Vinegar
  • 8 cloves Garlic, crushed
  • 4 pieces Dried Bay Leaves
  • 1 tbsp Peppercorns
  • 1 1/2 cups Water
  • 1 tbsp Brown Sugar
  • 2 tbsp Cooking Oil

Instructions

1

Combine pork, soy sauce, and garlic in a bowl. Marinate for at least 1 hour (overnight is best).

2

Heat oil in a heavy pot. Brown the pork pieces in batches to lock in flavor.

3

Pour in the marinade, water, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil.

4

Simmer on low heat for 45-50 minutes until the pork is very tender and the fat has rendered.

5

Add vinegar and sugar. Do not stir for 3 minutes to let the vinegar cook off.

6

Continue to simmer uncovered until the sauce reduces to an oily, savory glaze. Serve with hot rice.
