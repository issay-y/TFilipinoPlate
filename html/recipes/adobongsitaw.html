Adobong Sitaw

A healthy and budget-friendly Filipino favorite. String beans sautéed with garlic, onions, and often pork bits, simmered in a salty-tangy adobo sauce.

Prep: 10m
Cook: 15m
Servings: 3
Try meatier versions:
Pork Adobo Chicken Adobo Adobong Puti

Ingredients

  • 300g String Beans (Sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 100g Pork Belly, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 1/4 cup Soy Sauce
  • 3 tbsp Cane Vinegar
  • 5 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 small Onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 1/2 tsp Ground Black Pepper
  • 2 tbsp Cooking Oil

Instructions

1

Sauté garlic and onions in oil until fragrant. If using pork, add it and cook until browned.

2

Add the sitaw (string beans) and stir-fry for 2 minutes.

3

Pour in the soy sauce and water. Cover and simmer for 5 minutes until the beans are tender but still crisp.

4

Add the vinegar and black pepper. Do not stir for 2 minutes to let the vinegar cook off.

5

Give it a final stir and cook until the sauce is reduced. Serve hot.
