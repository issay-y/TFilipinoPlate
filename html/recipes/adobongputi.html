Adobong Puti

The ancestral way of cooking Adobo. By omitting soy sauce and using salt and extra garlic, the natural flavor of the pork and the tang of the vinegar shine through.

Prep: 10m
Cook: 50m
Servings: 4
View other versions:
Pork Adobo Chicken Adobo Adobong Sitaw

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Pork Belly (Liempo), cubed
  • 1/2 cup White Cane Vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Sea Salt
  • 10 cloves Garlic, crushed
  • 3 pieces Dried Bay Leaves
  • 1 tbsp Whole Peppercorns
  • 1 cup Water
  • 2 tbsp Cooking Oil

Instructions

1

Marinate the pork in vinegar, salt, and half of the garlic for at least 30 minutes.

2

Sauté the remaining garlic in oil until golden brown. Add the pork and sear until lightly browned.

3

Pour in the marinade, water, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Bring to a boil.

4

Simmer on low heat for 40-45 minutes until the pork is tender and the liquid has reduced.

5

Continue to cook until the sauce turns into a clear, savory oil. The pork should be slightly crispy.

6

Adjust salt to taste and serve with warm garlic rice.
